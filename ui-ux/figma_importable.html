Interactive Lecture Slide Deck

SLIDE 01

AVL Rebalancing Overview

An AVL tree is a self-balancing binary search tree where the height difference of any node's left and right subtrees must be at most 1.

Formula: BF = Height(Left) - Height(Right) ∈ {-1, 0, 1}
SLIDE 02

Single Tree Rotations (O(1))

Rotations rebalance nodes by exchanging pointer assignments in constant time.

  • Single Left (LL): Balance factor exceeds +2 on left child.
  • Single Right (RR): Balance factor drops below -2 on right child.
SLIDE 03

Double Tree Rotations (O(1))

Applied when a single rotation is insufficient due to inner imbalance paths.

  • Left-Right (LR): Double rotation resolving left-right grandchildren paths.
  • Right-Left (RL): Double rotation resolving right-left grandchildren paths.

UI/UX Design Psychology Spec

This canvas hosts interactive specimens of key design components and documents the professional psychological rules applied across the interface for immediate review and approval.

Design Psychology Rules Implemented

1. Fitts's Law (Generous interactive targets)

All button click zones are designed to exceed a minimum of **44x44px** (such as our 50px circle zone). Hover interactions raise buttons slightly (`-2px`) and enlarge shadows to show affordance. Active clicks trigger a mechanical scale-down (`scale(0.95)`) to simulate tactile resistance.

2. Miller's Law & Hick's Law (Chunking structure)

Instead of overwhelming the student, sources are chunked logically into structured "card lists" (e.g. mock Google Drive list) with high white-space padding to minimize cognitive friction and visual noise during critical study flows.

3. Gestalt Laws (Similarity, Proximity, Continuity)

Similar elements share unified geometries (e.g., active tabs are rounded pills with high-contrast shadows). High proximity binds label headers directly to their inputs. Visual continuity is maintained through the top dynamic breadcrumb indicating exactly which tab is active.
